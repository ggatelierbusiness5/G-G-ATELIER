The inspiration

A surface that
waits for light.

The Lake Club Polo is designed around an evening colour that is not quite navy and not quite green—the lake-dark tone remembered after the sun has left Udaipur.

Its proposed jacquard stays within that narrow range. Pattern is created by the way the surface catches light rather than by a contrasting print. From across a room the polo appears almost solid; at closer distance the texture begins to move. A stone-toned collar provides a quiet edge without breaking the nocturnal mood.

The current direction calls for a dense hand and precise athletic line. The deeper surface is intended for resort evenings, travel and refined city dressing. Final fibre content, jacquard construction, measurements and colour standards remain subject to production confirmation.

Design notes

Deep-midnight ground

02

Tonal jacquard

Texture supplies the motif, revealing and receding as the wearer moves through changing light.

03

Stone restraint

A softened neutral at the collar lifts the face while preserving the composition’s low contrast.

04

Limited signature

The smallest proposed G&G mark leaves the garment’s surface and silhouette in control.
