The destinations

Five chapters.
One India.

A resort wardrobe imagined through atmosphere: coastal light, palace geometry, lake-dark evenings and mountain restraint.

G&G
01
Goa Riviera

Sun-faded leisure.

Chalky whites, weathered terracotta and soft marine blue recall long lunches, shaded verandas and the easy formality of a coastal club. The proposed prints are airy and geometric rather than tropical cliché.

View the polo selection
02
Kerala Coast

Backwater calm.

Ivory, mist sage, deep navy and a trace of water blue translate the quiet movement of the Kerala backwaters. Palm forms become restrained rhythm across cloth, softened enough to live beyond a holiday.

Meet the Backwaters Polo
03
Jaipur Heritage

Palace geometry.

Sandstone beige, antique brass and olive draw from Jaipur’s measured courtyards and latticework. The architecture informs scale and repetition; it is not reproduced as a literal palace illustration.

Meet the City Palace Polo
04
Udaipur Club

Lakeside evenings.

Midnight green, stone and quiet silver evoke Udaipur after dusk. A tonal jacquard rewards changing light and close attention, giving the polo presence without relying on a loud surface print.

Meet the Lake Club Polo
05
Himalayan Escape

Mountain restraint.

Mineral grey, alpine green and winter cream suggest rarefied air and quiet layers. Texture leads the story: fine ribs, subtle melange and dense collars designed to feel composed in cooler settings.

The method

Inspired by place.
Designed for life.

Each collection begins with a feeling, not a souvenir.

We study proportion, light, landscape and material memory, then reduce them into a palette and repeat. Cultural references are handled as design research and atmosphere; G&G Atelier does not claim affiliation with the places, palaces or institutions that inspire a chapter.

01

Observe

Find the emotional detail: filtered light, softened stone, water at dusk or the rhythm of a screen.

02

Distil

Remove the obvious until only colour, spacing, texture and a restrained original motif remain.

03

Wear

Test the idea against a timeless wardrobe, an athletic silhouette and the realities of repeat use.
