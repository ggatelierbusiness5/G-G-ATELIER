Why 220 GSM polos
can feel premium.

Fabric weight can create substance, coverage and drape. The quality of the fibre and construction decides whether it becomes refinement.

GSM means grams per square metre. It tells you how much a square metre of fabric weighs. It does not tell you whether the cotton is long-staple, the yarn is smooth or the collar will recover after wear.

That distinction matters. “220 GSM” sounds precise and is useful for comparison, but it is a measurement of mass—not a certificate of quality. A premium polo emerges when appropriate weight meets good fibre, controlled knitting, balanced finishing and considered garment construction.

What does 220 GSM mean?

At 220 GSM, a polo fabric usually sits in a substantial middle-to-heavier range, depending on knit type and finishing. It may feel more structured than a lightweight summer jersey and can support a cleaner silhouette. A 240 GSM construction increases mass further, though a 20-gram difference alone does not tell you how either fabric will wear.

GSM can also change through finishing and moisture conditions. Comparing numbers is most useful when the fabrics have similar fibre content, knit and test conditions.

Why can it feel refined?

Body: additional mass can help the polo fall away from the body instead of clinging to every contour. That supports the composed, athletic silhouette G&G Atelier is exploring.

Coverage: a well-built midweight fabric may be less transparent, especially in ivory or white. This can make pale polos easier to wear without layering.

Surface: enough yarn can produce a clear piqué texture or a dense, smooth jersey face. When the yarn is even and the finish controlled, the cloth can feel substantial without being coarse.

Stability: a balanced knit may hold the placket, hem and shoulder line with less collapse. Stability still depends on loop construction, seam engineering and finishing—not weight alone.

Weight creates the possibility of substance. Fibre and construction determine whether that substance feels luxurious.

What GSM cannot tell you.

Two 220 GSM polos can feel completely different. Shorter fibres may create more surface fuzz. Loosely controlled yarn can distort. Heavy chemical softening can feel impressive at first touch and diminish after washing. An unstable rib collar can curl even when the body cloth is dense.

GSM also does not reveal colourfastness, shrinkage, pilling resistance or dimensional recovery. Those properties need appropriate testing and real wear. G&G Atelier will not present a weight number as proof of performance; final claims should follow confirmed production specifications.

Is 220 GSM suitable for India?

Comfort depends on more than ambient temperature. Humidity, airflow, personal preference, knit porosity, fit and activity all matter. An open piqué can move air differently from a compact jersey of the same weight. A slightly relaxed athletic fit can feel cooler than a tight fit because fabric is not pressed against the skin everywhere.

For hot, humid days, prioritise breathability and ease. For air-conditioned interiors, travel or evening wear, additional body may feel reassuring. No single GSM suits every Indian climate or wearer.

How to assess a polo in person.

  • 01Look across the surface. The knit should appear even, without unintended streaks, thin patches or excessive fuzz.
  • 02Compress and release. The cloth should regain shape rather than remaining crushed or feeling rigid like board.
  • 03Stretch gently. Watch whether the fabric and collar return, while recognising that laboratory recovery is a separate measurement.
  • 04Inspect the structure. Shoulder seams, placket, collar and hem show whether the fabric has been handled thoughtfully.
  • 05Ask for the complete specification. Fibre, knit, weight, finish and care matter together.

The useful question is not “Is 220 GSM premium?” It is “What has this maker done with 220 GSM?” A compelling answer should be visible in the hand, drape, recovery and honesty of the product description.
